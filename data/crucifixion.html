Jesus Christ

This page presents a neutral, factual overview of the life of Jesus of Nazareth and a summary of how different religious traditions understand him. Historical details are separated from theological claims where appropriate.

I. Life of Jesus Christ

Birth and Early Life

  • Jesus (Yeshua in Hebrew) is described in the Gospels as being born in Bethlehem and raised in Nazareth.
  • Mary is described as his mother; Joseph is described as his legal guardian.
  • Most scholars place his birth around 4–6 BCE during the reign of Herod the Great.
  • Outside of the Gospel accounts, almost no independent historical information exists about his childhood.

Beginning of Public Ministry

  • He began public teaching around age 30.
  • He was baptized by John the Baptist and then preached throughout Galilee and Judea.
  • Key themes: the Kingdom of God, repentance, love, forgiveness, moral renewal.
  • Methods attributed to him include teachings, parables, healings, exorcisms, and reported miracles.
  • He gathered disciples, traditionally numbered at twelve.

Conflict and Opposition

  • His teachings and actions created tensions with some Jewish authorities and with Roman political authorities.
  • He was accused of blasphemy (by Jewish authorities) and sedition (by Roman authorities).

Crucifixion and Burial

  • Jesus was crucified under Pontius Pilate, a well-attested historical fact.
  • Date is usually placed between 30–33 CE.
  • He was buried in a rock-cut tomb according to the Gospel accounts.

Resurrection Reports

  • Followers reported that he rose from the dead and appeared to many.
  • This claim is central to Christianity; its truth is a matter of faith, not something historians can verify.

II. How Different Religions View Jesus

Christianity

  • Jesus is the Son of God and the Messiah.
  • Beliefs include virgin birth, miracles, atoning death, bodily resurrection, ascension, and return.

Judaism

  • Jesus is not recognized as the Messiah or as divine.
  • He may be viewed as a teacher or reformer, but his followers’ claims are not accepted.

Islam

  • Jesus (Isa) is one of the greatest prophets.
  • He is honored, born of a virgin, performs miracles by God’s permission, and is not crucified according to mainstream Islamic teaching.

Hinduism

  • Views vary widely: teacher, saint, or avatar in some interpretations.

Buddhism

  • Not part of Buddhist doctrine, but often seen as a compassionate moral teacher.

Baha’i Faith

  • Jesus is a Manifestation of God and a true divine messenger.

Secular Historical Perspective

  • Historians agree Jesus existed, preached, gained followers, and was crucified.
  • Supernatural claims are treated as matters of belief rather than historical fact.
